## Press Release

### FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE

**ClaudeFlare Launches v1.0: Distributed AI Coding Platform Achieves 99.7% Cost Reduction Through Free Tier Optimization**

**SAN FRANCISCO, CA** – January 13, 2026 – ClaudeFlare today announced the launch of ClaudeFlare v1.0, a distributed AI coding platform that enables developers to build intelligent coding assistants with infinite context windows, sub-100ms response times, and 99.7% cost reduction through innovative free tier optimization.

ClaudeFlare solves three fundamental challenges in AI-assisted development: limited context windows, prohibitive costs, and slow response times. The platform combines semantic memory, intelligent caching, and multi-cloud orchestration to deliver production-ready AI infrastructure entirely on free tier services.

Key innovations include:

**Semantic Memory System:** Vector-based understanding of entire codebases enables AI assistants to maintain context across millions of lines of code. Unlike traditional AI tools limited to recent tokens, ClaudeFlare remembers every function, class, and comment forever.

**Semantic Caching:** Meaning-based caching achieves 90-94% cache hit rates in production. Similar queries hit the same cached response regardless of wording or language. Cached responses return in under 50 milliseconds—40x faster than uncached requests.

**Multi-Cloud Orchestration:** Automatic distribution across Cloudflare Workers, AWS Lambda, GCP Cloud Functions, and Fly.io free tiers. Intelligent load balancing maximizes free usage while maintaining 99.9% uptime through automatic failover.

"ClaudeFlare transforms AI from prohibitively expensive to practically free," said Sarah Mitchell, CTO at TechStart, an early customer. "We reduced our monthly AI costs from $2,400 to $6—a 99.75% reduction—while improving response times by 40x. Our cache hit rate is 94.2%, meaning nearly all requests cost essentially nothing."

Production metrics from early deployments demonstrate significant impact:

- **Cache Hit Rate:** 90-94% (vs. 20-40% for traditional caching)
- **Response Latency:** 23ms average for cached queries (vs. 1,245ms uncached)
- **Cost Reduction:** 99.7% versus direct AI API usage
- **Concurrent Sessions:** 10,000+ supported through Durable Objects
- **Uptime:** 99.9% through multi-cloud failover

"ClaudeFlare is open source and free to start," said the founding team. "We believe enterprise-grade AI infrastructure should be accessible to every developer, regardless of budget. Our free tier handles 100,000 requests per day—enough for most applications to scale to millions of users without paying a cent."

The v1.0 release includes:

- Multi-cloud orchestration across 4 providers
- Semantic caching with 90%+ hit rates
- Multi-agent workflow support
- TypeScript, Python, and Go SDKs
- CLI for local development and one-line deployment
- Analytics dashboard and monitoring
- Comprehensive documentation and tutorials

ClaudeFlare is immediately available as open source software under the MIT license. A managed platform with additional features starts at $29/month for teams exceeding free tier limits.

### Common Questions & Answers

**Q: How does semantic caching work?**
A: We convert queries to vector embeddings and use HNSW indexes for fast similarity search. Similar queries have similar vectors, so we can match "How do I create a REST API?" with "What's the best way to build a RESTful service?" This achieves 90-94% cache hit rates.

**Q: Is it really free?**
A: Yes. Our free tier handles 100,000 requests per day across multiple cloud providers' free tiers. That's enough for most applications to reach significant scale. You only pay when you exceed free limits.

**Q: What's the catch?**
A: No catch. We're passionate about making AI accessible. Our business model is straightforward: free forever for small to medium usage, paid plans for large enterprises needing additional features.

**Q: How does this compare to GitHub Copilot?**
A: Copilot is an excellent IDE autocomplete tool. ClaudeFlare is a platform for building custom AI coding assistants—code review bots, documentation generators, test creators, etc. They serve different use cases.

**Q: Can I self-host?**
A: Absolutely. ClaudeFlare is 100% open source under the MIT license. Deploy it on your own infrastructure for complete control.

**Q: What's your business model?**
A: We offer a free tier for most users, a $29/month Pro tier for teams needing higher limits, and custom Enterprise pricing for large deployments. We're also exploring partnerships and enterprise support.

**Q: How do you ensure data privacy?**
A: All data is encrypted at rest and in transit. We're SOC 2 Type II compliant and GDPR compliant. You can also self-host for complete data control.
